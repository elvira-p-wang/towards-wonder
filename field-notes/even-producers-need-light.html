Even Producers Need Light.

No. 004 Identity 7 May 2023 2 min read

TThe thought came to me while staring at a ranking list.

A thousand names.

Neatly ordered.

Within seconds, a number had become a verdict. Some people celebrated. Others quietly questioned everything they knew about themselves.

We often say that self-worth should come from within. That we shouldn't depend on recognition from other people.

I believed that too.

Until I realised something surprisingly obvious.

Even producers need light.

A tree can make its own food, but it cannot make its own sunlight. Without light, chlorophyll never forms. Without chlorophyll, there is no growth.

Perhaps people are not so different.

We do not need constant applause.

But if someone spends too long unseen, unheard, or unacknowledged, even the strongest sense of purpose begins to fade.

Maybe recognition was never the opposite of self-worth.

Maybe it is simply one of the conditions that allows it to grow.

That day, I wrote three reminders to myself.

Stay calm.

Stay angry.

Stay awake.

Calm enough not to confuse rankings with identity.

Angry enough to notice when a system mistakes measurement for meaning.

Awake enough to remember that numbers can describe performance, but never a person.

Sometimes the world feels strangely divided.

Half the people laugh as if nothing hurts.

The other half burn quietly, carrying dreams that no one else can see.

Ash is rarely meaningless.

It is simply what remains after something bright has spent a long time trying to become real.

Perhaps that is why I have never believed that success should mean becoming "better" than someone else.

If effort means anything at all, I hope it brings us a little closer to a world where fewer people need to stand beneath someone else's shadow just to feel worthy.

Because every tree needs light.

But sunlight was never meant for only a few.
