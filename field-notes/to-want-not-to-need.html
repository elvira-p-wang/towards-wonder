To Want, Not to Need.

No. 003 Relationships 30 April 2026 2 min read

PPeople often describe love as though it exists to rescue us from loneliness.

I have never believed that.

Loneliness is not something another person can resolve on our behalf. If I ask a partner or a friend to complete what I cannot bear to face alone, love quietly becomes dependence, and affection begins to carry the weight of obligation.

I hope to meet another person not as two halves searching for completion, but as two whole lives choosing to walk together.

To celebrate each other’s joys. To carry one another through grief. To laugh, to disagree, to grow, and to face the uncertainty of life side by side.

Not because either of us is incapable of standing alone.

But because some journeys become more meaningful when they are shared.

Perhaps that is the difference between needing someone and wanting someone.

To need someone is to say, I cannot be whole without you.

To want someone is to say, I am already whole, and I still choose you.

One day, my partner said to me,

“I don’t need you, but I want you.”

I have always thought there was something quietly beautiful about that sentence.

Not because it dismisses dependence.

But because it places choice above necessity.

Perhaps love is not about finding someone to complete us.

Perhaps it is the quiet decision of two people, already complete in themselves, to keep choosing one another.
