Two Ways of Loving Beauty.

No. 017 Art 8 July 2026 1 min read

TTravelling through Denmark and Italy, I found myself experiencing two very different philosophies of beauty.

In Denmark, beauty seemed woven quietly into everyday life. It softened ordinary moments, made public spaces feel gentler, and turned daily routines into something worth savouring. It felt calm, restrained, understated, almost effortless.

Italy felt different.

Its beauty carried the weight of history. It was solemn, passionate, and unapologetically grand. Walking through its streets felt less like inhabiting the present than stepping into a civilisation determined to leave something behind.

Neither felt better.

They were simply asking different questions.

One asked how beauty could make everyday life more humane.

The other asked how beauty could outlive us.

Perhaps that is why walking through Copenhagen and walking through Florence felt so different, even before I entered a museum.

One is quietly creating the future.

The other is lovingly preserving the past.
